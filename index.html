About

I am currently a master's student in Electrical Engineering at Pennsylvania State University. Before that, I received my B.Eng. in Automation from Ocean University of China. My background is in signal processing, machine learning, and computer vision.

Broadly, my research interests lie in the intersection of biomedical image analysis, machine learning, and computer vision, with a focus on designing interpretable tools and architectures that have potential in real-world clinical applications.

Research Interests

  • Biomedical image analysis and computational pathology
  • Interpretable and trustworthy machine learning
  • Computer vision and image processing

Research Projects

  • Etiology-Guided, Informed AI for Histological Evaluation of IBD Ongoing
    Developing an etiology-guided, interpretable multiple instance learning framework for histopathology image analysis in inflammatory bowel disease, combining domain priors, attention heatmaps, tissue segmentation, and ordinal regression for clinically meaningful severity scoring.
  • Intelligent Measurement Method for Blade Noise of Fast Wind Turbines
    Designed an intelligent monitoring pipeline that converts acoustic signals to spectrograms and applies deep learning and computer vision techniques to predict wind turbine blade conditions, using simulated fault noise to enrich the dataset.
  • ArUco Marker-based Indoor Localization System
    Built a 3D indoor localization system using ArUco markers to construct a global coordinate frame and estimate real-time camera pose, including cross-validation from multiple markers and joint estimation of distances and poses.
  • AI-based Movie Recommendation and Q&A System
    Contributed to an AI-powered movie recommendation and question-answering system by curating a Chinese movie dataset and integrating a ChatGLM2-6B language model with backend recommendation logic.
