About

I am currently a master's student in Electrical Engineering at Pennsylvania State University. Before this, I received my B.Eng. in Automation from Ocean University of China. My background is in signal processing, machine learning, and computer vision.

My research interests lie in the intersection of biomedical image analysis, machine learning, and computer vision, especially in designing interpretable tools and architectures that have potential in real-world applications.

Research Interests

  • Biomedical image analysis and computational pathology
  • Interpretable and trustworthy machine learning
  • Computer vision and image processing

Research Projects

  • Etiology-Guided, Informed AI for Histological Evaluation of Inflammatory Bowel Disease Ongoing
    Developed an etiology-guided, interpretable AI framework for histopathological evaluation of Inflammatory Bowel Disease, combining domain priors with deep learning to enable accurate and efficient automated analysis
  • Intelligent Measurement Method for Blade Noise of Fast Wind Turbines
    Developed an intelligent methodology for monitoring wind turbine blade status by integrating time-frequency analysis with deep learning and computer vision
  • ArUco Marker-based Indoor Localization System
    Developed an indoor localization framework by deploying ArUco markers to establish a global 3D coordinate system and enabling real-time pose estimation through marker detection and coordinate transformations.
